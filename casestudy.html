Nebula

A collaborative cloud storage app for the modern world.

Summary

I was asked to design and create a new brand identity for Nebula, a new cloud storage app. Nebula is a new, innovative app that brings social media to the cloud storage market to provide users with real time collaboration and to build a community.

My role on this project was to create a fresh, new brand to help make them stand out from the rest. I designed everything from the color scheme, the name and the logo.

Problem

The “problem” that I was trying to solve is the lack of real time collaboration in current cloud storage apps while trying to integrate a social media component to provide a sense of community to users.

Solution

The solution was Nebula. As the world changes and heads more towards technology, a shared interest community online and in real time is a great way to get into the ever growing world of cloud storage. Nebula can take a single thought or idea and create something bigger while simultaneously creating a network of friends and potential colleagues.

The Process

When I was approached to create an identity for this new cloud storage app, I knew that their target demographic included people who wanted to create and work together on projects in real time as well as people who wanted to work on solo projects, but maybe get ideas from other like-minded individuals. What started out with a simple idea to bring people together became Nebula.

Like all projects, we had to start somewhere. In this case, I started with research and analysis of existing apps and research of current and potential users of this new app. I then gathered all the information from surveys and created user personas and stories: what types of people I thought would benefit from a new cloud storage app like Nebula, and what those users would want from this app. Meanwhile, I was able to create a brand identity, color palette, logos etc, to come up with an overall look and feel of Nebula. From there, the pieces of the puzzle started to fit together. I was able to come up with wireframes, mockups, and, ultimately, prototypes that were tested both in person and remotely.

User Research

I started out by creating and sending out a user survey for people who currently use, or may use, cloud storage apps. I gathered all the responses and found that the most important features for a cloud storage app would be increased and affordable storage space and easier offline access. Other notable points were that a majority of people would like to comment on other’s work, have the ability to choose between a public and a private profile, and have this service be used both personally and professionally.

Competitive Analysis

The cloud storage market already has a few “big” names, however I found a great opportunity to delve into the market with Nebula.

I compared Google Drive, OneDrive, and DropBox. The biggest strengths of all three services include availability on multiple platforms (iOS, Android, web) and easy organization.

A major weakness for Google Drive was that you can only use Drive if you have a Google account. Furthermore, viewing and commenting on other’s work is not easily accessible without the file being shared with you.

DropBox’s weaknesses include not enough free storage and increased security risks.

OneDrive’s weaknesses include difficulty sharing files as well as vulnerability to hackers.

To address these issues with the competitors, Nebula provides more space for lower costs, allows users to browse public content and request access to private content, and makes it easy to share files with social media, links, and e-mails.

User Personas and User Stories

After going over the survey results and completing the analysis of existing cloud storage apps, I was able to come up with different user personas as well as user stories to paint a picture of what a potential user could want in a new cloud storage app. Using the data from the user surveys to create “real people” was crucial to forming a backbone and structure for this app.
